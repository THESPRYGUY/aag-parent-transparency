Parent Transparency Around Outside Speakers

A constructive parent-led request for clearer notice, age-appropriate review, and a written process when outside speakers or third-party presenters are brought into the school.

Why this matters

This is about process, trust, and age-appropriate transparency.

Outside speakers can add real value to student learning. Many families support thoughtful programming on important topics. The concern is not whether schools should ever discuss difficult subjects. The concern is whether parents are properly informed before sensitive content is presented to children, and whether there is a clear process for questions, review, and alternative arrangements where appropriate.

Some families have experienced presentations that left children confused or distressed. Other families may simply want advance notice, the speaker’s name, the organization, the topic, the materials, and how the presentation connects to curriculum or school goals.

Parents should not be finding out after the fact that outside presenters discussed sensitive topics with young children.

This request is not anti-school, anti-teacher, or anti-any-topic. It is a practical request for transparency and good governance.

Parent awareness

What families should know in advance

We are asking for a simple, consistent notice process when external speakers, guest facilitators, performers, or third-party organizations present to students.

  • speaker name and organization;
  • topic, focus, and learning objectives;
  • curriculum connection or school purpose;
  • grade level and age-appropriateness review;
  • slides, videos, handouts, books, giveaways, or other materials;
  • whether sensitive topics will be discussed;
  • who reviewed and approved the content;
  • how parents can ask questions in advance;
  • how parents can request an alternative activity where appropriate.
School council role

Why this belongs at School Council

School council does not manage the school, direct teachers, or approve curriculum. But school council is a parent-facing advisory and accountability forum. It exists so parents and members of the school community can contribute to student success, ask reasonable questions, and make recommendations to the principal and the board.

That makes school council the right place to discuss a school-wide process: advance notice, parent awareness, age-appropriateness, materials review, alternative activity options, and post-session feedback.

Proposed motion

Motion to present to School Council

The goal is to ask council to make a recommendation, not to accuse anyone or debate individual incidents.

Proposed Motion:

That School Council request the principal to provide the school’s current process for approving external speakers, guest presenters, performances, and sensitive-topic presentations; and that School Council recommend the creation of a written parent notification and transparency protocol for outside speakers, including advance notice, speaker details, topic, purpose, curriculum connection, materials, age-appropriateness review, sensitive-topic safeguards, and an opt-out or alternative activity process where appropriate.

The open letter

Open letter to AAG families

Hi everyone,

I wanted to share a respectful parent concern that I think deserves a broader conversation among Allan A. Greenleaf families.

When outside speakers, facilitators, or third-party organizations are brought into the school to speak with our children, parents should know about it in advance, understand what will be discussed, and have a clear process for asking questions or requesting an alternative activity where appropriate.

Our family has had experiences where outside presentations left us concerned about age-appropriateness and process. After trust was damaged, we advised the school that we wanted our child opted out of external speakers unless we had advance notice and could review the details. From what we understand, a similar speaker may later have been brought back, and it appears our request may not have been properly followed.

I am not raising this to accuse anyone of bad faith. I am raising it because these situations should not depend on informal memory, hallway conversations, or parents finding out afterward. They should be handled through a clear, written, transparent process.

This is why I believe School Council should ask for a simple external speaker and sensitive programming transparency protocol.

The goal is clarity, not conflict. Transparency protects students, supports staff, and rebuilds parent trust.

Tone commitment

Constructive engagement

This effort is intended to be respectful and process-focused. It is not about targeting staff, naming individual presenters, or turning a school issue into a political fight.

Families can support important learning while also asking that sensitive material be handled with clear parent communication, age-appropriate review, and consistent accountability.
